Fourier Series Explorer

The script can be found here.

Please note: This project was very a sequal to my original Fourier Series Explorer. The information on this page will make a lot more sense if that page is viewed first.

Explanation:

After making my Fourier series explorer for a student. It got me thinking about where else I could apply the equations. I realised that any parametric equation that defines a closed loop must be periodic on the parameter variable. I played with the maths and found that a 2D parametric shape could be described by two Fourier series equations of the forms x(t), y(t) where t is the parameter.


The beauty of this is that, like for normal Fourier series, the 2D doesn't have to be smooth for the equations to accurately approximate the shape. This means that 2D shapes with vertices can be modelled with equations that are very friendly to analysis. Through playing around, I also notices that the approximations don't seem to suffer from the Gibbs phenomenon that plague the normal use of Fourier series. Though, I'm not sure why this is off the top of my head!


How it works:

The script logic works very similarly to the Fourier Series Explorer. The only real difference is in the mathematics. The parametric data for each line is split into two functions x(t) and y(t). The Fourier series algorithm is then applied to both separately to make a parametric Fourier series which is then plotted at each of the chosen orders.
